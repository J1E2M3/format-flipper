Accessibility

Statement last reviewed July 6, 2026

Format Flipper aims to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Accessibility is part of the tool’s core promise: it should work for everyone, on any device, with any input method.

What’s implemented

  • All text colors meet or exceed the 4.5:1 AA contrast ratio on every background they appear on
  • Full keyboard operability — every control is reachable and usable without a mouse, with visible :focus-visible indicators, plus shortcuts (Ctrl/⌘+Enter to convert, Ctrl/⌘+Shift+S to swap)
  • A skip link and <main> landmark for jumping straight to the converter
  • Screen-reader support: labelled form controls, role="alert" plus aria-invalid/aria-describedby on parse errors, and a polite live region announcing converted output
  • A logical heading hierarchy (h1 → h2 → h3)
  • No motion beyond subtle hover transitions; no autoplaying or flashing content

Known limitations

  • Converting very large inputs happens synchronously and can briefly delay feedback on slow devices
  • The two large text areas rely on native browser textarea behavior for text navigation

Feedback

If anything here doesn’t work with your assistive technology, that’s a bug — please email toolymctoolface@gmail.com or open an issue on GitHub, and include the browser and assistive technology you were using.
